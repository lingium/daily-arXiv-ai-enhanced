### [1] [Evidence Synthesis in Probabilistic Extreme Event Attribution: From Attribution Measures to Model Parameters](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19516)
*Erik Haufs,Axel Bücher,Jonas Schröter*

Main category: stat.ME

TL;DR: 本文批判性评估了现有极端事件归因方法，提出改进措施和一种新的参数级合成方法，通过模拟研究和2024年9月暴雨案例验证了其有效性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有极端事件归因方法通常分别估计各数据源的归因度量再合成，存在潜在的缺陷。作者旨在识别这些不足并提出改进方案。

Method: 提出了针对基准程序的定向改进，以及一种新的参数级合成方法，该方法通过合并非平稳分布回归参数，实现对多个事件阈值和反事实气候条件下的推断。

Result: 模拟研究表明提出的改进显著优于基准程序，参数级合成方法展现出具有竞争力的整体性能。通过2024年9月Storm Boris强降水案例验证了实用性。

Conclusion: 参数级合成方法在极端事件归因中具有优势，能够更好地处理多阈值和不同气候情景下的推断问题。

Abstract: Probabilistic extreme event attribution aims to quantify how anthropogenic climate change has altered the likelihood or intensity of a class of extreme events. Existing studies commonly combine evidence from observational products and climate-model ensembles by first estimating attribution measures, such as probability ratios or intensity changes, for each data source and then synthesizing the resulting estimates. We critically assess this approach, identify potential shortcomings of a respective benchmark procedure from the literature, and propose both targeted modifications and a new parameter-level synthesis method. The latter combines estimates of the underlying nonstationary distributional regression parameters, thereby enabling inference across multiple event thresholds and counterfactual climate conditions. In controlled simulation studies, the proposed modifications substantially improve upon the benchmark procedure, while parameter-level synthesis provides competitive overall performance. The practical usefulness is illustrated through a case study of the heavy precipitation associated with Storm Boris in September 2024.

### [2] [Mens: Nonlinear shrinkage estimation in nonparanormal models for financial applications](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19825)
*Hamid Karamikabir,Mohammad Arashi*

Main category: stat.ME

TL;DR: 本文针对非偏正态（高斯copula）模型，提出了边际无关非线性收缩（MENS）协方差估计方法。该方法将oracle非线性收缩函数应用于正态得分秩协方差矩阵的特征值，在保持秩估计鲁棒性的同时实现非线性收缩的效率。理论证明了经验谱分布几乎必然收敛到广义Marchenko-Pastur律，以及MENS在Frobenius损失下的渐近最优性，并发现了尖峰潜在相变。模拟和S&P 500实证验证了其优越性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 高维协方差估计在金融等领域面临两大挑战：数据非正态性（偏斜、重尾）和高维带来的估计偏差。传统非线性收缩方法依赖于正态性假设，而rank-based方法虽然稳健但缺乏效率。作者希望在非偏正态模型这一更现实的半参数框架下，同时获得秩估计的稳健性和非线性收缩的统计效率。

Method: 提出MENS估计器：首先计算正态得分秩协方差矩阵（将每个观测值通过秩变换映射到正态分位数），然后对其特征值应用oracle非线性收缩函数，最后重构协方差矩阵。理论分析利用了随机矩阵理论中的广义Marchenko-Pastur律和Baik-Ben Arous-Peche相变。

Result: 推导了正态得分协方差经验谱分布到广义Marchenko-Pastur律的几乎必然收敛性；证明了MENS在旋转等变估计类中Frobenius损失下的渐近最优性；发现了尖峰潜在相关性的BBP相变。模拟研究验证了边际不变性和尖峰相变。S&P 500最小方差回测中，MENS相比线性收缩得到更良态的协方差估计、更低的样本外组合波动率和更低的换手率。

Conclusion: MENS成功将非线性收缩从高斯设定推广到非偏正态模型，统一了秩方法的稳健性与非线性收缩的效率。该方法对高维金融资产配置等实际决策任务具有重要实用价值。

Abstract: We develop a theory of nonlinear shrinkage covariance estimation for nonparanormal (Gaussian-copula) models, in which each observed coordinate is an unknown strictly increasing transformation of a latent Gaussian vector. This model accommodates arbitrary marginal skewness and heavy marginal tails while retaining a Gaussian dependence structure, and it is the natural semiparametric setting for heavy-tailed, asymmetric financial returns. Our estimator, marginal-free nonlinear shrinkage (MENS), applies an oracle nonlinear shrinkage function to the eigenvalues of the normal-scores rank-covariance matrix. We give the almost-sure convergence of the empirical spectral distribution of the normal-scores covariance to the generalized Marchenko-Pastur law of Sigma, and asymptotic optimality of MENS among rotation-equivariant estimators under Frobenius loss. We establish a Baik-Ben Arous-Peche phase transition for spiked latent correlations. The MENS attains the robustness of rank-based estimation and the efficiency of nonlinear shrinkage at once within this class. We corroborate the theory with a simulation study that isolates the marginal-invariance property and the spiked transition. In an out-of-sample minimum-variance backtest on S&P 500 stocks, MENS delivers a better-conditioned covariance estimate, lower realized portfolio volatility, and lower turnover than linear shrinkage, illustrating its practical value for high-dimensional allocation and decision-making.
